13. Savoring with Breaks

Paradox: Interrupting a positive experience increases total enjoyment.
sequenceDiagram
    participant C as Continuous
    participant B as With Breaks
    Note over C: Eat chocolate non-stop
    C->>C: Piece 1 (Great!)
    C->>C: Piece 2 (Good)
    C->>C: Piece 3 (OK)
    C->>C: Piece 4 (Meh)
    C->>C: Piece 5 (Barely notice)
    Note over C: Total enjoyment: LOW (adapted)
    Note over B: Eat with intentional breaks
    B->>B: Piece 1-2 (Great!)
    B->>B: 10 min BREAK (do something else)
    B->>B: Piece 3-4 (Great again! Reset!)
    B->>B: 10 min BREAK
    B->>B: Piece 5 (Still good!)
    Note over B: Total enjoyment: HIGH (reset each time)
Key: Breaks reset hedonic adaptation. Spacing out positive experiences preserves their novelty and pleasure.

15. Free Time vs. Happiness

Too little AND too much free time reduce happiness. Sweet spot: 2-5 hours/day.

pie title Where Is Your Mind?

28. Flow: Challenge-Skill Model

Flow happens when challenge matches skill. It occurs more at WORK than leisure!

Exam Tip: Flow conditions: clear goals, immediate feedback, challenge = skill. Flow at work > leisure because work naturally provides goals, feedback, and challenge. Paradox: people want more leisure but experience more flow at work.
